> **Idea:**
> Once you have an idea you are happy with, that you want to take to the next level: grab all of your notes on it and write it in a document.
> 
> That's going to be where you're going to be starting from - all those loose ideas and you're gonna use them for the next stage.
> It's important to treat it as a living document because it's something you're gonna refer to over and over and over again. 
> 
> **The Outline:**
> For this stage of the process don't worry about making all the different branches, were gonna focus on just one main branch where you are gonna wanna hit all the major story beats that you want to hit over the course of your story. What are story beats? You're gonna have lots of like little scenes, little ideas—things that you're gonna want to hit over the course of the story. So, for your main branch go ahead and just pick out a whole bunch of moments that you think really work well together. Put those along a line and then figure out ways of how to get from one scene to another. Now this part is kind of loose, it's kind of free form. There is lots of different ways people are going to be writing their outlines but I find this method works really well.

>  How to Write Visual Novels: A Step-by-Step Guide
> 
> This YouTube video outlines a comprehensive process for writing visual novels, emphasizing a flexible, iterative approach.  The creator stresses the importance of starting with a solid idea, even if it's initially vague, and iteratively refining it throughout the process.

> **Key Points:**
> 
> 1. **Ideation:** Begin with a core idea (character, plot, setting, or theme). It's crucial to be selective and discard ideas early if they don't show promise. Define your target audience early on.
> 2. **Outline:** Create a loose outline focusing on the main story branch, hitting major story beats.  Consider how gameplay (if any) will integrate with the narrative. Develop character profiles, focusing on motivations and flaws. Create a living document, subject to change.
> 3. **Drafting:** Write a chronological first draft, updating the outline as needed. Pay close attention to character motivations to avoid writer's block. Expect significant changes during this stage.
> 4. **Editing:**  Cut unnecessary content, tighten dialogue, and streamline the narrative. Aim for a lean, polished story before adding complexity.
> 5. **Branching Narratives:**  Develop branching paths by revisiting the core idea and identifying recurring themes or motifs. Create new branches using the same process as the main branch (outline, draft, edit), using flags to track changes between scenes. Maintain consistency across branches. Consider the desired weight of different endings (e.g., equal weight in a romance VN).
> 6. **Gameplay Integration:** Plan gameplay elements after outlining the branching narrative to avoid wasted effort if gameplay proves unsuitable.
> 7. **Twig Stage:** Add optional content (side stories, details, etc.) that enrich the narrative without being essential to the main plot. These reward dedicated players without penalizing those who skip them.
> 8. **Collaboration (Multiple Writers):** Divide work collaboratively, ensuring consistent integration of different sections. Regularly review and update the outline and character profiles.

### Material

> Competition is the first force that makes companies treat customers with respect and makes them want to offer a good product. Unfortunately, 40 years ago, the US stopped enforcing the competition law and has been promoting monopolies ever since. 
> 
> **Competition also prevents lobbying**, which becomes possible if a lot of companies merge into one. 
> 
> **Regulation is the second force** - but only if the regulators are more powerful than the ones that are being regulated. When the company is bigger than the government, it gets hard to credibly threaten it. 
> 
> The companies nowadays are not constrained by competition and regulation - they can buy both of them out. 

![](https://i.imgur.com/wOmPJ5t.png)


> An app is just a web page skinned with the right kind of IP to make it a felony to put an ad blocker in it. In the EU they are actively trying to make it a federal law that this cannot be. 
> **When they do it, it's "progress", when you do it, it's "piracy".** 

![](https://i.imgur.com/Bwg6WGB.png)


> Tech lost it fear of competition, it neutralised the threat from its regulators and then it put those regulators in harness to attack new startups who might do onto them as they did unto everyone who dominated before them. 
> 
> But even then, there was one force that kept our bosses in check, and that was us. Tech workers. There was always a low supply of them, which the bosses knew and to make them work crazy hours, they called them pioneers, hired set designers and architects to design campuses with free laundromats, kombucha making workshops, meditation retreats etc. They convinced them that they were being pampered, instead of working like government mules. Unfortunately, the supply caught up with the demand - last year they fired 260,000 tech workers, so the ones that stayed have to comply to what the product's shitty vision is, instead of holding the ethical line. 
> 
> If we want to build a new, good internet, that our bosses can't wreck, we need to make sure that those constraints are durably installed on our new internet, wound around its roots and nerves. We have to stand guard over those forces, so that they can't be dismantled again. A new, good internet is one that has the positive aspects of the old, good internet, the ethic of technological self-determination where users of technology and the hackers, the tinkerers, the startups and the others who serve as their proxies, can reconfigure the technology to make it work, so that it does what they need it to do and it cannot be used against them.


> You know who lives in the future - Amazon warehouse workers - they are living in your future. Drivers whose eyeballs are monitored by AI cameras, that do phenology on their faces to figure out when to dock their pay and warehouse workers whose bodies are ruined in a matter of months. 
> 
> Amazon warehouse workers have to piss in bottles and have the highest rate of on the job industry injuries of any comparable company in the industry, whereas Amazon coders get to show up for work with facial piercings, green Mohawks and black t-shirts that say things their bosses don't understand. They can piss whenever they want to - that's not because Jeff Bezos loves tech workers, it's because they're scared you'll quit and they won't be able to replace you.
> 
> If you want to go on fighting for the user, you need power that's better than scarcity - you need a union. 

> We can create a new good internet, that we're going to need to fight fascism, the climate emergency and genocide, to build a digital nervous system for the 21st century, in which our children will thrive and prosper.

> Hrathen: “You will find that hate can unify people more quickly and more fervently than devotion ever could.”

**Context from the book:**
Hrathen, a gyorn - a high Derethi priest, was sent to Arelon to convert it's people to the religion of Shu-Dereth. He decided to unify them against their Korathi-believing rulers (and thus against the Korathi religion as a whole) by making them hate the Elantrians - people who formerly were considered near Gods, since they were immortal and able to perform miracles like healing. They have fallen from grace because of the mysterious curse called Shaod - any pain they felt stayed with them forever, slowly driving them into madness. Hrathen knew that the people of Arelon already envied the Elantrians and some felt good about finally being better than them - he just had to ramp that up to a full-blown hatred and then use it as a tool to direct it wherever he wanted. 

**Real-life application:**
This is something that a lot of political/ruling bodies do - whether that be parties, religions, companies or lobbyists. They distract their target population by unifying it against an easy target - usually marginalised groups that differ from them visually, by behaviour, by cultural practices or by ways of living, by language or by quality of life. Since these groups cannot fight back (since they are already marginalised and in a losing position), they are an easy target and usually there is quite some fears/worries/unpleasant opinions already surrounding them that are easy to blow out of proportion. This is because marginalised groups are also often alienated and do not mix into the general population. 

**My project application:**
In my project I want to show how the system uses silent changes, like banning books or changing the language or making us hate the people who want the language to change and accommodate everyone. Every beginning of a conversation about language is met with criticism from both sides - the right and the left, saying that we should focus on "bigger problems" but then never actually addressing them and shutting those who try to. Maybe one of characters that would represent the system (maybe the boss of the character at her job?) could say something like Hrathen did or quote him? I think it's a powerful quote - maybe one of the main character's friends could say it to her to show her that she shouldn't work there?

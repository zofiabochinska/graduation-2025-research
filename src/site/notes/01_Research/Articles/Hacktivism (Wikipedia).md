### Material

> A form of [Internet activism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_activism "Internet activism") with roots in [hacker culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hacker_\(programmer_subculture\) "Hacker (programmer subculture)") and [hacker ethics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hacker_ethics "Hacker ethics"), its ends are often related to [free speech](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_speech "Free speech"), [human rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights "Human rights"), or [freedom of information](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_of_information "Freedom of information") movements.
> 
> [Hyphanet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyphanet "Hyphanet"), a [peer-to-peer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peer-to-peer "Peer-to-peer") platform for [censorship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Censorship "Censorship")-resistant communication, is a prime example of translating political thought and [freedom of speech](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_of_speech "Freedom of speech") into code.
